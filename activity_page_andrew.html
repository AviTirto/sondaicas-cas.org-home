Project Description

There are only sixty eight Javan Rhinos left in the whole world, all of them exist in the boundaries of Ujung Kulon National Park on the south-westernmost tip of Java. While being a critically endangered species, there are some good news in the form of two newborns months only after the death of Samson in May of 2018 signing a slow yet positive trend in their wellbeing. Even then, the population still faces a multitude of challenges such as Aregna Obtusifolia (Langkap) overpopulation. The invasive species of plants leeches on high standing trees and closes the forrest canopy ultimately resulting in death for plants like tall grass and short trees, vital sources of nutrients for Javan Rhinos. If nothing is done, this positive trend of increasing population size is going to end very soon. This is why we at sondaicus is cooperating with the team at Ujung Kulon National Park in a program to mediate langkap growth while simultaneously providing jobs to residents of nearby Pandeglang. In theory, the process is simply to remove a certain percentage of the population as to reduce reproduction and yet, the reallity of the situation is much more complex, Ujung Kulon itself is divided into geographic squares that are about 8 kilometer in length making keeping record on approximate location and cataloging progress extremely difficult as shown by a map of one geographic block focusing on the areas of Langkap growth. This is why we need your support, a donation will greatly help us grow the team and improve better record keeping to overcome this challenge and help the conservation of the Javan Rhinos.
